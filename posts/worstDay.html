Did you remember that 4/18/2025 was the day that I bought snorkel? It was such a good day, I picked up a rare Toyota SUV that was in pristine condition for an absolutely killer price. The journey to mens night, the trip to Rochester to watch the documentary about Face of Jesus. I still have snorkel added on my carfax account, so I get the service updates done to it. As of 6/6/2026, it crossed the 300,000 miles mark. I sold it when it was at 287,000.

Exactly one year after buying it, 4/18/2026, things took a turn. It was supposed to be a good day. It started out like any other Saturday: wake up, shower, have breakfast, and clean the apartment. The day before, I saw a Facebook post giving a small local bait shop a shoutout. It's run by an elderly 81 year old man plus his family, but the older fella is the one that runs the daily operations of the shop. I wanted to go support him. Like who doesn't love supporting small businesses, especially if they are run by elderly people! Anyways, the shop was 26 minutes away from my apartment. Since it was a pretty cool day, I took Gibby with me - he enjoys the car rides. I got very close to the shop, a little over a mile away and then boom. This happens.

THE FRIGGIN ALTERNATOR DIES ON ME AND GIBBY!!! Like brother, all that talk from me about Toyota/Lexus never leaving me standed. It just left me and Gibby standed in the middle of an intersection. YES! It died in the middle of an intersection. Thankfully the middle of the intersection was a high point, so when it died it slowly rolled forward to the opposite side of the intersection and then I put the car in park and just stood there, helplessly. I called a tow truck to come pick it up and then called my sister to come pick me up. She was in the middle of cooking and couldn't leave immediately. So once the tow truck took my car away, Gibby, I and my water bottle walked the remaining of the way to the bait shop. It was a nice walk, I spent about 45 minutes at the shop chatting with the old man and his life. My sister finally came to pick Gibby and I up, and we went back home. As soon as I got home, I FRIGGIN realized that I left my water bottle at the FRIGGIN BAIT SHOP that was almost 30 minutes away. Lucky me, I have two cars. So I got right back into the Tundra, parked inside a garage and drove back to the bait shop to get my water bottle. Once I got my water bottle, I went to Scheels. In my last fishing journey with Milos, I forgot to carry back the fishing net to his car, so I lost it. I've always used the same net, it folds up nicely and the holes are small enough for me to catch bait fish with it. Due to FRIGGIN inflation, the net use to be $30, but now it's $50! Like how fun. Speaking of fun, did you know that this is the 3rd time that I've had to' buy this same net? The 1st time, my uncle stepped on it, the second time I broke it in my kayak, and then again on 4/18/2026. I was already having a bad day, and then my favorite fishing net doubles in price... Oh well, I'll live. I finally get back to the apartment, park my tundra in the garage. I grab everything except for the FRIGGIN water bottle. Now, it was a windy day, so I had on a hat + the hood on my hoodie was up, so my peripheral vision isn't great. I hit the button on the inside of my garage to close the door, turn around , quickly realize that I forgot the water bottle again, and then turn around immediately and boom. I smack my forehead against the bottom of the garage door as it was coming down. My face was dented. No pics there.
